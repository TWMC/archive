Nemoto-san (Tochigi-ken)

Nemoto-san (1,199 m), situated in the Kiryu region south of Nikko, where you are more likely to see bears than people, is a sacred mountain associated with the Black Tengu and samurai, and with a notoriety as a place for gambling. On the, perhaps, little dangerous route to the summit, is Nemoto shrine, an amazing wooden structure perched high above a cliff, and closer to the summit there are original tsuga (reddish pines), quite unique to Japan. With dense mixed forest and thick bamboo grass in places, the route may be difficult to follow. Map time: 5 1/2 hours; difficulty: ***

Paul Reay

Asahi-dake(Fukushima-ken)

Asahi-dake in Nasu is a popular destination for family outings and is also said to be visited regularly by the Royal Family. On the first day, we will hike from Nasu-sanroku to Santo-goya onsen via Chausu-dake where we will have a view of the active volcano. Santo-goya onsen used to be a busy lodging village in olden days. The hut there is a rarely preserved 130 year old large sturdy wooden structure. On the second day, we will climb Asahi-dake (1896 m), and walk along Okura ridge down to Hita Onsen for dip in a large open-air onsen that resembles a swimming pool. The views from the summit are gorgeous all round, and vegetation along the way may also be noteworthy. Difficulty: **

Ryokami-yama (Saitama-ken)

On a previous hike, a peculiar shaped mountain whose ridge looked like teeth of saw attracted our attention. Later, we identified that the mountain as Ryokami-yama (1,724 m), one of the most popular mountains in Chichibu area. This is a 1.5 day hike, with a 6.5 hr walking time. We will leave Tsukuba in the late morning, stay in a mountain hut at the foot of Ryokami-yama, and climb the peak on the 2nd day. If we are lucky, we may encounter interesting mushrooms on the trail. As there are rocks and long chains near the peak, I will cancel the walk if the conditions looks too severe (ice or snow). In this case, I'd like to organize a one day trip to Bukka-san (747 m), a lower mountain in the Tanzawa area. Difficulty: ***

Masako Okamoto

Akagi-san - Around the Caldera (Gunma-ken)

Mount Akagi is an ancient sacred mountain, and the recognized symbol of Gunma prefecture. It commands the entire sweep of the Kantou plain, which spreads out in a fan at its feet. The mountain is, in fact, an ancient volcano with three crater lakes. We will hike around one of these three lakes. Difficulty: ***

Nicolas Delerue

Chirtsmas walk & party (Tsukuba-shi)

When the Christmas bell rings, and before all our members go for vacation, the TWMC gets ready to celebrate Christmas. Traditionally, the Christmas party consists of two parts, a walk up and down to Tsukuba-san (876 m) wearing Santa robs/caps, and a party in the evening. The walk is very easy and suitable for all ages. If the weather is clear, and in winter it is so very often, there may be splendid views of Fuji-san, the mountains around Nikko, and also the Shinjuku skyscrapers from the summit. After the walk we will go to an onsen and return to Tsukuba for the party at Hot Stuff.

Hakone (Kanagawa-ken)

Hakone, a land of steaming vents, onsens, and a picturesque lake, was previously a large conical volcano resembling the present-day Fuji, but its central part collapsed leaving the triple volcanic cones which are seen today. For this easy one day walk we will be climbing Komoga-take (1327 m). From the summit, the views of Ashinoko Lake, Izu Seven Islands and Suluga Bay are really great.

The trip is planned for those who are interested in downhill skiing or snowboarding in Inawashiro, Fukushima. Takao-san (Tokyo-to)

This range of low mountains, with peaks below 1,000 metres, offers year-round walking to Tokyo's millions. With its well laid out paths, it is an excellent area to get a first taste of walking in Japan, or for more experienced walkers to keep fit. The walk will coincide with the fire-walking ceremony (Hiwatari Matsuri) at Yakuou Temple, situated near the Summit of Takao-san (600 m). Difficulty: **

Nicolas Delerue

27-28

Tanzawa (Kanagawa-ken)

Rising abruptly from the Kanto Plain, the Tanzawa Mountains are the nearest peaks over 1,000 metres to the sprawling conurbation of Tokyo and Yokohama. With its fine mountain scenery, Tanzawa offers the possibility of year-round walking, and magnificent views of Fuji-san during the colder months. A good place for novice walkers, but some routes offer a challenge even to the more experienced. Difficulty: ***.

Paul Reay
