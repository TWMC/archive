Okutama

We will be doing the leisurely walk from Hinatawada station to Hinode Yama (902m), then visiting the Odake cave. This claims 300m or so of underground caves with all the usual geological features. From there we shall walk up to Mitake-san (929m), a popular site with a spectacular shrine on the summit. Depending on time, we shall then have the option of either walking down or taking the cable car. This is an easy walk, rated **, everyone welcome..

Nasu: Asahi-dake (Fukushima-ken)

Asahi-dake (1903m) is one of the peaks that make up the Nasu mountain range where the crown prince and princess are said to vacation regularly. The paths from the ropeway station are well maintained. The views from the summit are gorgeous all round if blessed with good weather, with the active Chausu volcano to the south. Vegetation along the way may also be noteworthy. Then a long gentle descent along Okura ridge will take us down to Kita-onsen for a dip in a large open-air onsen. See the pictures from the two-day walk in 2003 to the area at
http://eve.bk.tsukuba.ac.jp/twmc/previous_walks/2003_11_Asahi/Asahi2003.html
Difficulty: **

Kamikochi

 Kamikochi is regarded as the "Birthplace of Japanese Alpinism" and, if you enjoy the outdoors, a visit to the area in the noble grandeur and magnificence of the Kita (North) Alps is a "must do". Situated in the southern section of the Kita Alps, Kamikochi is dominated by the highest, and most impressive peaks of the range. The area also provides, bearing in mind that this is a three day walk along 3,000 m peaks, a number of route options to suit all levels of ability. The two walks meet at the spear-like Yari-ga-take, one of Japan's most distinctive and famous peaks, on the afternoon of the second day, and everyone will walk together on the final day. The Walks: Both walks take in at least one of Fukada Kyuuya's 100 Famous Mountains, as well as some in the Top 200. As the final day is common. Difficulty ** to ****.
 

Paul

Oze

 At an altitude of 1,400 m, Oze, part of the Nikko National Park, is the largest mountain marsh in Japan, the peaks of Hiuchi-ga-take (2,360 m) and Shibutsu-san (2,228 m) at its East and West ends offering wonderful panoramic views. Famed for its mizubashou, unflatteringly called skunk cabbage in English, in the Spring, and also popular in the Autumn, I thought it was about time to visit the marsh during the Summer. With wooden planked trails laid out over the
marsh, and two of the Top 100 mountains to climb, there's something for everyone. This two day trip will include an overnight stay at one of the huts in the marsh area. Difficulty: ** to **** depending on which route you take.

Paul

Yatsu-ga-dake

Legend has it that Yatsugatake was once higher than Fuji, but Fuji rose and kicked its rival over to form the eight peaks of the range. Now long dormant, the range still shows remnants of its volcanic past along its rocky ridge.On Saturday we will climb the highest peak on the range, Akadake (2,899m) ―a reasonably gentle walk culminating in a rather spectacular but easy rocky scramble to the peak. We will stay overnight in one of the huts just below the peak. The next day we will follow the ridge northwards over two further peaks, Yokodake (2,829m) and Iwodake (2,742m). This part of the walk is a little more adventurous and, weather permitting, there are spectacular views over the valleys below, and of the Japan Alps to the west and south. We can enjoy the panorama over the volcanic crater at Iwodake before descending back down the valley to our starting point, where we can have a bath before driving home, possibly stopping for dinner on the way. This walk is rated ***/****.

Sumon & Asakusa-dake (Nigata / Fukushima-ken)

The idea of this walk is to enjoy autumn in a quiet mountain region. Sumon-dake (1537m) and Asakusa-dake（1586m）, locate on the border of Nigata and Fukushima prefectures, are the ideal mountains for this purpose. The combination of heavy snow falls and ancient volcanic activities have created a variety of landscapes in this region. We will walk through gentle beach forest that harbors wilderness including luminescent mushrooms and bears, to the grassy peak with marshes, viewing rocky ridges of surrounding mountains along the ascent. From the summit there are great views of Hiuchi (Oze), Nasu, Bandai and Ide mountains. As it is planed for a three day weekend, there may be an option to stay an extra night in a mountain hut, making it a three day trip.
Difficulty: ***
