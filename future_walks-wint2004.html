Kumotori Yama

Kumotori-yama, the "Cloud Grabber", one of Fukada Kyuuya's 100 Famous Mountains, sitting right on its border with Saitama and Yamanashi-ken, is Tokyo's highest peak at 2,018 m. Located in the Chichibu-Tama National Park, the nearest national park to Tokyo, this two day walk will start in the western region of Okuchichibu, and finish in the eastern region of Okutama. Difficulty:***
 

Paul

21

Okutama

Okutama lies to the west of Tokyo, and is popular for its year-round hiking options. Although the autumn foliage will be in decline by this time, the clear winter air should offer spectacular views of surrounding peaks, including Mount Fuji, and the trail will take us over the scenic Tama River,
past traditional thatched houses, the popular Mitake shrine (founded 200 years ago and containing national treasures) and even possibly a stalactite cave. The exact course will be chosen closer to the time depending on weather conditions and club preferences, but a likely course would take us over the peaks of Hinode-yama (902m) and Mitake-san (929m), and either Otsuka-yama (921m) or the longer and more strenuous option of Odake-san (1267m) and Nokogiri-yama (1047m). We will be going by train, so everyone is welcome to join! Difficulty ** (or *** depending on the course)
 

Ginny

Christmas walk and Party

When the Christmas bell rings, and before all our members go for vacation, the TWMC gets ready to celebrate Christmas. Traditionally, the Christmas party consists of two parts, a walk up and down to Tsukuba-san (876 m) wearing Santa robs/caps, and a party in the evening. The walk is very easy
and suitable for all ages. If the weather is clear, and in winter it is so very often, there may be splendid views of Fuji-san, the mountains around Nikko, and also the Shinjuku skyscrapers from the summit. After the walk we will go to an onsen and return to Tsukuba for the party at Hot Stuff. Difficulty: *
 

Terry

Izu

Izu is the peninsula to the west of Tokyo and roughly south of Fuji-san. It is characterised by a mild winter climate, numerous onsens and a special variety of sakura (cherry) trees. The first day will take us to the highest part of Izu. The plan is to walk the not overly undulating ridge encompassing Mansaburou-dake (1,406m) and Manjirou-dake (1,300m). If the weather plays along there should be very nice views of Fuji-san. The second day will see an easy stroll along the seashore at the southernmost tip of Izu. This area should be the most remote part of the peninsula, with nice views of the shore. We will visit seaside onsens on 2nd day. Difficulty: **
 

Akira Kagawa

Animal tracking-hiking at Okutama

Okutama area is full of great mountains, lush pine forests and a variety of animals. In the mountains we hardly get chance to meet animals so we generally do not realize there are other creatures. This time we adopt a different kind of approach towards nature, which combines animal tracking and hiking (it is important in terms of safety reason as well). On the first day of this event animal-tracking program will be organized. We will be hiking some peaks around there later on the same day and the second day. After this we will also visit an onsen. difficulty **/***

X-country Skii

This is the third time for this trip. The pension hotel is very nice with a friendly owner. You have immediate access to a X-country circuit. The owner can also drive you to the nearby Liza World ski resort. And it is 15 minutes by car from the 3-valley zao resort. Snow-shoes can also be rented and therefore this trip offers you various possibilities. Beginner/Intermediate downhill ski on Liza World (and you remain on your ski all the way down to the pension at the end of the day). All round ski on Zao. On top of Zao, you will be able to see the famous snow monsters. Ski rentals are available at the pension village.

Tanzawa (Kanagawa-ken)

Rising abruptly from the Kantou Plain, the Tanzawa Mountains are the nearest peaks over 1,000 metres to the sprawling conurbation of Tokyo and Yokohama. Tanzawa, with its fine mountain scenery, is a good place for novice walkers, but some routes offer a challenge even to those more experienced. Hiru-ga-take (1,673 m), one of the Top 100, is the highest peak in Tanzawa with great views in all directions, and is particularly noted for it's fine views of Fuji-san. There will be two options for fit and leisure walkers with both groups meeting and spending the night in the hut at the top of Tou-no-dake. Easy option will start in Oo-kura and finish below Oo-yama on the other day, whereas the difficult option will start below Oo-yama and finishing at Nishi Tanzawa Kyoushitsu. Difficulty: ** (easy option) or *** (difficult option)
 

Richard Wasserbauer

Hokyo-san hiking and Sumi-E painting

This hike is aimed at families/children, to let children discover the fun of walking in the nature. Others are also encouraged to join this walk. For this walk we will go to Hokyo-san, the small mountain in 'front' of Tsukuba-san. It is a small mountain with several trails as options, some easier than the easy. We might see the seasonal Katakuri flowers. In this hike we will also introduce Sumi-E painting (Japanese ink painting) and try to sketch katakuri flowers.

April

29

Atago-san plus Kasama Pottery Fair

This is a local walk; the destination is only 50 km north-east of Tsukuba. The peaks we are going to visit are Atago-san (306m) and Nantai-san (553 m), which offer excellent views across to Tsukuba-san, Kasumi-ga-ura, and the Kantou Plain. We might come upon wild lily bells (suzuran) blooming beside the trail. From Dourokushin Pass, we will walk down towards Kasama where the Himatsuri Festival, the biggest pottery fair of the year in this pottery town is held.
 

Masako
