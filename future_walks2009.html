 Mount Nokogiri hiking and tokyo bay swimming

*One-day trip, depending on the weather. Mount Nokogiri is located in west Chiba, near the south part of Tokyo bay. The mountain got its name from its characteristic saw-shaped profile of a Japanese saw (, nokogiri). It falls steeply into Tokyo bay on its western side, where we can have a good viw of the tokyo bay.  and the western side is the very famouse Nihon Temple, which is the home of the largest Daibutsu sculpture in Japan. We can have a look at the typical japanese style buddist temple there. Distance: about 10km, 3 hours walk. Difficulty: **/***

July 25-26

 Mt. Fuji

The hike will begin on Saturday morning. Departure will be leisurely at around 8AM or 9AM. Travel will be by train and bus from Kawaguchiko (or possibly train and bus from Shinjuku, but the firs option is easier). We will arrive at Kawaguchiko at about midday, and from there we will catch the bus to 5th station. After lunch at 5th station, the climbing will begin horizontally. After a few hours of climbing, we will stop at 8th station (about 3200m altitude) for dinner and a long break to acclimatise. We will begin climbing again at about 12AM to 2AM, which should get us to the top or close to it in time for the sunrise. Breakfast can be eaten at the top, after which a walk around the crater to the true summit and moon base on the far side and back again will take about one hour, before beginning our long descent back to 5th station.

Geoff Biggs
