Kaba-san

Kaba-san locates right here in Ibaraki-ken, not so far away from Tsukuba-san. We plan two options, one is car based (usual route!) and the other is cycling trip. The car based trip (Amabiki kannon-Kaba-san-Tabacoo shrine) will start at Rakuhouji Temple, about 2 km north of Kaba-san, also known as Amabiki Kannon, is regarded as one of the most beautiful temples of the Bandou pilgrimage. The walk will follow a largely well maintained path along the ridge from above Rakuhouji Temple as far as the spectacular craggy peak and shrines of Kaba-san (709 metres), then will descend by the route used by worshipers to more shrines at the bottom. One of the shrines is the Tobacco Shrine, featuring the largest
pipe in Japan! The path will be steep in places, but very safe, with no cause for concern. The cycling trip (cycling-Kaba-san- doll festival-cycling) will start from Tobacco Shrine.

Kita Alps for beginners!

This walk is for everyone who wants to experience the thrill of high mountains and spectacular views afforded by the North Japan Alps without having to tackle any of the area ’s more strenuous and sometimes nerve-wracking routes. On Saturday we will start from Kamikochi, walking alongside a crystal river overlooked by high peaks and continuing up a long, gentle ascent that only steepens on the final approach to Yarigatake. The next morning you will have the chance, if you wish, to complete the rocky scramble to the peak of Yarigatake (3,180m) or explore part of the ridge ―or merely enjoy the spectacular views in all directions before descending down the valley on the other side through a beautiful meadow of flowers. This is Kita Alps made easy! You will also have the opportunity to attempt a more challenging route on TWMC’s next walk in mid-July. **/***

Kita Alps for the Dedicated!

Kamikochi is regarded as the "Birthplace of Japanese Alpinism". Situated in the southern section of the Kita (North) Alps, Kamikochi is dominated by the highest, and most impressive peaks of the range. The area also provides, bearing in mind that this is a three day walk along 3,000 m peaks, a number of route options to suit all levels of ability. The two options (one "easy" option) in this walk meet at the spear-like Yari-ga-take, one of Japan's most distinctive and famous peaks, on the afternoon of the second day, and everyone will walk together on the final day. Both walks take in at least one of Fukada Kyuuya's 100 Famous Mountains, as well as some in the Top 200. As the final day is common. Yatsu-ga-dake

Legend has it that Yatsugatake was once higher than Fuji, but Fuji rose and kicked its rival over to form the eight peaks of the range. Now long dormant, the range still shows remnants of its volcanic past along its rocky ridge. On Saturday will climb the highest peak on the range, Akadake (2,899m) ―a reasonably gentle walk culminating in a spectacular but easy rocky scramble to the peak. The next day we will follow the ridge northwards over two further peaks, Yokodake (2,829m) and Iwodake (2,742m). This part of the walk is a little more adventurous and, weather permitting, there are spectacular views over the valleys below, and of the Japan Alps to the west and south. We can enjoy the panorama over the volcanic crater at Iwodake before descending back down the valley to our starting point. ***/****

Kinjyo-san and Makihata-yama (Niigata/Gunma-ken)

This hike is going to be a combination of cultural experience and nature exploration. On the first day, we will visit Kinjyo-san (1369m), which is a sanctuary belonging to Unto-an-temple, a major Soto-sect Zen training centers. Although it is not a high mountain, the rocky peak clothed in autumn color has beauty and the Buddhists' sculptures on the peak add mystical power. On the second day, we will clime Makihata-yama (1,967m), a high mountain that is located at the back of Kinjyo-san. To climb this mountain, we will follow the Nukubi-sawa rapids alongside (sometimes inside) the water until its origin, with several waterfalls on the way. Chains are fixed in the most severe places, but not all the slippery parts are supported. After we reach the origin, we will climb up to the main trail toward the peak of Makihata-yama. As opposed to the bit nerve-wrecking former half, we will be able to enjoy a relaxing grassy ridge near the peak. Map time and difficulty: 6 hrs walk, *** for Kinjyo-san, 8.5 hrs walk, **** for Makihata-yama.
