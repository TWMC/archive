Sumon & Asakusa-dake (Nigata / Fukushima-ken)

The idea of this walk is to enjoy autumn in a quiet mountain region. Sumon-dake (1537m) and Asakusa-dake（1586m）, locate on the border of Nigata and Fukushima prefectures, are the ideal mountains for this purpose. The combination of heavy snow falls and ancient volcanic activities have created a variety of landscapes in this region. We will walk through gentle beach forest that harbors wilderness including luminescent mushrooms and bears, to the grassy peak with marshes, viewing rocky ridges of surrounding mountains along the ascent. From the summit there are great views of Hiuchi (Oze), Nasu, Bandai and Ide mountains. As it is planed for a three day weekend, there may be an option to stay an extra night in a mountain hut, making it a three day trip.
Difficulty: ***

Mitsu-toge-yama (1785 m)

 This is a day hike to get one of the best views of Fuji-san, a panorama of the north side from its summit to its forested foothills, and the surrounding lakes. We will start at Mitsu-toge-station and climb Mitsu-toge-yama passing 88 little buddhas dressed in red aprons and a rock climbing wall (We will not climb, just pass the wall ;-) ). If we are lucky with the weather, from the top of Mitsu-toge-yama we will have great views to Fuji-san, Yatsu-ga-take, Tanazawa, and the lakes Sai-ko and Shoji-ko. The descent first leads through a grassy meadow, and then through a forest. Finally, we will arrive at Kawaguchi-ko, where we will visit an onsen. The hike is rated easy to medium (or ** to ***); it is an 6-7 hours hike, and 14 km long.

Christmas walk and Party

When the Christmas bell rings, and before all our members go for vacation, the TWMC gets ready to celebrate Christmas. Traditionally, the Christmas party consists of two parts, a walk up and down to Tsukuba-san (876 m) wearing Santa robs/caps, and a party in the evening. The walk is very easy and suitable for all ages. If the weather is clear, and in winter it is so very often, there may be splendid views of Fuji-san, the mountains around Nikko, and also the Shinjuku skyscrapers from the summit. After the walk we will go to an onsen and return to Tsukuba for the party at Hot Stuff. Difficulty: *

Izu (Amagi-san)

Izu is the peninsula to the west of Tokyo and roughly south of Fuji-san. It is characterised by a mild winter climate, numerous onsens and a special variety of sakura (cherry) trees. If the weather plays along there should be very nice views of Fuji-san. The second day will see an easy stroll along the seashore at the southernmost tip of Izu. This area should be the most remote part of the peninsula, with nice views of the shore. We will visit seaside onsens on 2nd day. Difficulty: **

X-country Skii

Cross country skiing is a pleasant way to enjoy winter. Although it may not be as exciting as downhill skiing, with cross country skiing, you can enjoy the beauty of a winter scene at your own pace. It is easy to learn, very good exercise, and it doesn't hurt as much when you fall down.
On the weekend of February 17 and 18, TWMC will go to the Nikko area for cross country skiing. Close to Senjogahara (a famous Edo era battlefield), there are many prepared ski trails suitable for any level skier. It is also possible to make your own trails on Senjogahara.

Kaba-san

Kaba-san (709 m), located just ""behind"" Tsukuba-san, is famous for its"religious status, and is known as a place for monks to practice asceticism. On this trip, you can enjoy a relatively easy walk, visiting the impressive Rakuhou Temple (Amabiki Kannon), and seeing several miniature shrines along the ridge.

Family walk

This hike is aimed at families/children, to let children discover the fun of walking in the nature. Others are also encouraged to join this walk.
