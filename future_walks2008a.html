10th Anniversary walk to Nikko

 The hike is a very easy but pretty walk. We plan to start at Lake Chizenjiku and walk up to the smaller but very pretty lake. We will be walking upwards the river connecting the two
lakes which is walking along a series of waterfalls including a 300 meter long lava bed 'waterfall', a number of smaller waterfalls. Afterwards we will walk around the smaller lake and end close to the Yumoto onsen. For those who do not wish to enjoy the onsen after the hike, there is another option to walk a bit further upwards to the next lake and come back to join the onsen-goers. Total map time is (map-depending) around 3 hours plus an optional 2 hours, however, I expect it to take quite a bit longer as we might have to stop for picture taking many times. Difficulty: */**

Introduction to Kita Alps

This walk is for everyone who wants to experience the thrill of high mountains and spectacular views afforded by the North Japan Alps without having to tackle any of the area ’s more strenuous and sometimes nerve-wracking routes. On Saturday we will start from Kamikochi, walking alongside a crystal river overlooked by high peaks and continuing up a long, gentle ascent that only steepens on the final approach to Yarigatake. The next morning you will have the chance, if you wish, to complete the rocky scramble to the peak of Yarigatake (3,180m) or explore part of the ridge ―or merely enjoy the spectacular views in all directions before descending down the valley on the other side through a beautiful meadow of flowers. Difficulty:***

Nakasendo walk & hike in Nagano

 Nakasendo is one of the old roads constructed in Edo era to connect Edo (Tokyo) and Kyoto through mountainous area. Along the route, there are post towns to provide travellers with accommodation and drinks/foods that still preserve the traditional architectures. In addition to the walk, we will enjoy hiking in the nature nearby. Difficulty: */**
